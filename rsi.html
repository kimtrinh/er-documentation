🚨 Difficult Airway Alerts
⚠️ Obesity (BMI ≥30)
• Use IBW for rocuronium/vecuronium
• Use actual body weight for succinylcholine
• Ramped positioning — ear to sternal notch
• Pre-oxygenate longer (↓ FRC, ↓ apneic time)
• Video laryngoscopy preferred
• Have bougie ready
🤰 Pregnancy
• Left lateral tilt (15°) — relieve aortic compression
• Rapid desaturation — ↑ O₂ consumption
• Difficult airway more likely (airway edema)
• Full stomach — highest aspiration risk
• Succinylcholine safe in pregnancy
• Avoid prolonged apnea — fetal hypoxia risk
• Ketamine safe; avoid large propofol doses
🚨 Predicted Difficult Airway
• Call for backup / senior now
• Prepare video laryngoscope as primary
• Have bougie, smaller ETT (6.0–6.5), LMA ready
• Cric kit open and at bedside
• Consider awake intubation if time allows
• LEMON assessment: Look, Evaluate 3-3-2, Mallampati, Obstruction, Neck mobility
• Don't paralyze if unsure you can intubate

🔪 Surgical Cric — Can't Intubate / Can't Oxygenate
1
Call for help. Position neck extended.
2
Identify cricothyroid membrane — midline, between thyroid and cricoid cartilage.
3
Scalpel: Horizontal stab incision through skin and membrane. Hook — tracheal hook to stabilize trachea, pull caudally.
4
Bougie: Insert bougie through incision, advance into trachea (feel rings).
5
Tube: Railroad 6.0 cuffed ETT over bougie. Inflate cuff. Confirm with waveform capnography.
6
Ventilate. Secure tube. Reassess. Call surgery for definitive airway.
