## 1. EM ‚Äì Internal Medicine (IM) Agreements

---

### üü¢ EM‚ÄìIM Non-Member Management Agreement
**File:** `IM and EM Non-member Management 6-29-22.docx`
**Date:** June 16, 2022 (~4 years old)
**Signatories:** Michael Schwartzwald MD (COS EM) & Junaid Sultan MD (COS Hospital Medicine), Kaiser SBC

**Key Rules:**

- **Stable non-member needing IM admit:** EM physician contacts ED case manager, places "discharge planning consult: stable nonmember transfer" order, initiates lateral transfer, completes H&P, and speaks to accepting physician.
- **Bed available:** EM signs transfer paperwork; may endorse to oncoming EM if shift ending.
- **No bed / no ETA:** EM may endorse to oncoming EM OR consult IM depending on complexity/acuity.
- **Wait > 4‚Äì6 hours:** EM may consult IM for ongoing management and/or KP admission.
- **Hand-off rule:** Patient formally transfers to IM team when initial EM attending goes off shift. EM continues signing out to incoming EM until then.
- **IM helps with:** Long-LOS patients (GI studies, PEG, IR procedures), complicated boarders, rare KP member transfers, potential AMCAH discharges.
- **If EM unsure about stability for transfer:** IM may be consulted for second opinion. If both agree patient is stable, EM continues transfer. If IM initiates transfer, IM owns ongoing management and transfer paperwork.
- **Escalation:** Disagreements escalate to administrative physician on call for each department.

**Agents covered:** Dabigatran (Pradaxa), Rivaroxaban (Xarelto), Apixaban (Eliquis), Edoxaban (Savaysa)

| Drug | Reversal Agent | Dose |
|---|---|---|

### üî¥ Outpatient DVT Anticoagulation Protocol
**File:** `Kaiser Outpatient Anticoagulation DVT Protocol 1.16.14.doc`
**Date:** January 16, 2014 (~12 years old) üî¥

**OACS (Outpatient Anticoagulation Service) Eligibility:**

**Inclusion:** Acute DVT (upper or lower extremity), age >18.

**Exclusion (hospitalize if present):**
- Active bleeding / high hemorrhage risk
- Stroke in past 6 weeks
- Non-cutaneous surgery in past 2 weeks
- Platelets <75,000
- Signs/symptoms of PE
- Morbid obesity (>150 kg)
- Heparin allergy / HIT history
- Pregnancy
- Already on therapeutic warfarin

**Enoxaparin Dosing (preferred):**
- Standard (CrCl >30): 1 mg/kg actual BW Q12h (preferred) or 1.5 mg/kg Q24h (home health only, ‚â§100 kg)
- Severe renal impairment (CrCl <30): 1 mg/kg actual BW Q24h; consider heparin if on dialysis
- Max weight: 150 kg for Q12h dosing; 100 kg for Q24h dosing
- Round to nearest available syringe strength (30/40/60/80/100/120/150 mg)

### üü¢ Stroke in COVID-19 Patients Management
**File:** `COVID 19 Ischemic Stroke Management in COVID patients V2.docx`
**Date:** ~2020‚Äì2021 (COVID-19 era) üü¢

**Key points:**
- COVID-19 patients have increased stroke risk (systemic inflammatory/pro-thrombotic state).
- Ischemic stroke incidence ~5%, ICH ~0.5%, venous sinus thrombosis ~0.5% of COVID+ patients.
- Risk factors: older age, HTN, DM, prior ischemic disease, elevated CRP, elevated D-dimer, severe COVID.
- Even young asymptomatic patients can have severe LVO strokes.

**IV tPA in known COVID-19:**
- **Must wait for PLT, PT, PTT** before giving IV rtPA (DIC risk / higher bleeding).
- RAPID Abbott testing for all stroke patients ‚â§60 with embolic-appearing or confirmed embolic stroke (including pre-thrombectomy) ‚Äî even without classic COVID symptoms.
- Note: After IV tPA, D-dimer rises ‚Äî account for this when using D-dimer for systemic prognostication.

**Additional workup:**
- D-dimer (quantitative DVT order), Anti-phospholipid Ab panel (lupus anticoagulants, beta-2 glycoprotein Ab, anti-cardiolipin Ab), Fibrinogen.
- CT preferred over MRI for logistical ease.

**Therapeutic anticoagulation indications (duration ‚â•4 weeks):**
- Embolic stroke unexplained by other mechanism / in setting of hypercoagulable state.
- Positive antiphospholipid antibodies.
- Severely elevated D-dimer >3,000 (especially >10,000).
- Consult Hem/Onc, ID, IM for interdisciplinary guidance on duration.

### üü° Neurosurgery ED Transfer Agreement (OMC ‚Üí FMC)
**File:** `Neurosurgery ED Transfer Agreement.docx`
**Date:** December 14, 2016 (~9 years old) üü°
**Signatories:** Alp Arkun MD (COS EM) & Todd M Goldenberg MD (COS Neurosurgery)

**Process:**

1. OMC ED physician identifies acute neurosurgical emergency.
2. Calls neurosurgeon on call immediately.
3. Asks specifically: **"Does this patient require or may potentially require an immediate life or limb saving neurosurgical intervention?"**

- **YES** ‚Üí Activate emergent neurosurgical transfer; neurosurgeon expects patient at FMC ED within **20‚Äì30 minutes**.
- **NO** ‚Üí Joint decision on disposition (hold at OMC, admit to OMC ICU, transfer elsewhere, etc.)

**Emergent Transfer Steps:**
- OMC ED physician notifies OMC Charge RN and FMC ED physician.
- OMC Charge RN calls **911** for emergent transfer (similar to STEMI process).
- OMC Charge RN notifies FMC Charge RN.
- OMC Charge RN attempts to secure ICU bed (does NOT delay transfer).
- Designated OMC ED RN accompanies ALS EMS crew (CCT not used ‚Äî too slow).
- FMC ED physician assigned and neurosurgeon paged upon patient arrival.
- Neurosurgeon at bedside **within 10 minutes** of FMC arrival.

### üü¢ Peds Sickle Cell Fever Protocol
**File:** `Peds Sickle Cell Fever.docx`
**Date:** January 31, 2022 (~4 years old) üü¢
**Author:** David Simon MD, Pediatric Hematology Oncology, KP Downey (P: 562-657-2360)

**Applies to:** SS, SC, S0 or S+ thalassemia patients
**Fever defined as:** >38.3¬∞C / >101¬∞F or subjective fever per parent.

**All febrile sickle cell patients require workup AND parenteral antibiotics.**

**Initial evaluation:** Vitals with pulse ox, history (compliance with prophylactic amox/PCN if <5 yo), physical (jaundice, lung exam, spleen span), immunization review.

**Labs:** STAT CBC with diff, retic, blood culture; STAT CXR if respiratory symptoms; UA/UCx if UTI suspected; rapid flu (if in season).

**Management:**
- **Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IV/IM (max 1g)** ‚Äî give ideally **within 1 hour**, do NOT wait for labs.
- O2 if sat <95% ‚Üí maintain >95%.
- Wheezing, hypoxia, or positive CXR ‚Üí start **Azithromycin** (first dose 10 mg/kg PO/IV, max 500mg).
- Albuterol PRN for wheezing.
- Influenza positive ‚Üí add Tamiflu.
- Septic/ill-appearing ‚Üí notify accepting hospital to add **Vancomycin**.

**Admit for ANY of:**
- Ill-appearing
- Fever >40¬∞C (104¬∞F)
- Respiratory distress, hypoxia, or abnormal CXR
- Age <12 months
- Age <24 months with incomplete immunizations or non-compliance with prophylaxis
- WBC <5,000 or >30,000
- Hgb <6 or 2 g/dL below baseline
- Social concerns (unable to follow up next day, no phone)

**After labs result:** Page pediatric hematologist on call.

### üü¢ Necrotizing Fasciitis (NSTI) Service Agreement
**File:** `Necrotizing Fasciitis Service Agreement.docx`
**Date:** December 22, 2021 (~4 years old) üü¢
**Signatories:** Carie McVay MD (COS General Surgery), Lawrence Albinski MD (COS Ortho), Stephen West MD (COS Plastics), Kaiser SBC

**Diagnosis:**
- Fulminant NSTI = clinical diagnosis ‚Üí DO NOT DELAY for workup.
- If clear on presentation: first available surgeon ‚Üí OR immediately.
- Hemodynamically stable with diagnostic uncertainty: work up with LRINEC score.

**LRINEC Score:**

| Lab | Points |
|---|---|
| CRP >150 mg/L | 4 |
| WBC 15‚Äì25 | 1 pt; >25 = 2 pts |
| Hgb 11‚Äì13.5 | 1 pt; <11 = 2 pts |
| Na <135 | 2 |
| Cr >1.6 | 2 |
| Glucose >180 | 2 |

- Score **>6**: Very concerning for NSTI.
- Score **<5**: Can help rule out if exam/imaging reassuring (cannot be used alone to rule out).

**Imaging:** US (quick, higher false-negative rate) or CT with IV contrast. **Air in soft tissues on CT = highly specific for NSTI ‚Üí immediate OR**.

**Document clinical photo in HealthConnect** for all consults.

**Consultation algorithm by anatomy:**
- **All NSTI regardless of location:** Consult **General Surgery** first.
- **Distal to elbow:** Gen Surg consults Hand Surgery (Ortho/Plastics) for assistance.
- **Distal to knee:** Gen Surg consults Orthopedic Surgery for assistance.
- **Foot only (NF or not):** Consult **Podiatry** (M‚ÄìF 0800‚Äì1700). After hours ‚Üí Orthopedics. Gen Surg available as backup.
- **ICU management:** General Surgery is primary.
- **Once stable ‚Üí floor:**
  - Below elbow ‚Üí Hand team takes over until discharge.
  - Below knee ‚Üí Orthopedics takes over until discharge.
  - Above elbow/knee ‚Üí General Surgery remains primary.
- **Soft tissue coverage (skin graft/flap):** Consult Plastics once wound is clean.

**Non-NSTI soft tissue limb infections:**
- Proximal to elbow or knee ‚Üí General Surgery
- Distal to elbow ‚Üí Hand Surgery
- Distal to knee ‚Üí Orthopedics

### üü° Bariatric Surgery CT Scan Protocols
**File:** `Kaiser Bariatric Surgery CT Scan Protocols.docx`
**Date:** Not dated (estimated 2015‚Äì2019) üü°

**Early Post-op (<30 days):**
- Oral contrast: 100mL over 1 hour, then 30mL on the gantry.

### üü° Pit Viper Snake Envenomation ‚Äî CroFab Protocol
**File:** `Pit Viper Snake Envenomation Management using antivenin Crofab Protocol.docx`
**Date:** Not explicitly dated (estimated 2018‚Äì2022) üü°

**Applies to:** Rattlesnake, sidewinder, or copperhead envenomation.
‚ö†Ô∏è If family brings a dead snake: DO NOT handle if head is attached ‚Äî pit vipers can envenomate reflexively post-mortem.

**Signs of envenomation:** Swelling/tenderness/bruising/bullae at bite site, fasiculations, hypotension, remote bleeding, metallic taste, vomiting, AMS, angioedema/airway compromise.
- 10‚Äì20% of bites are "dry" (no envenomation).

**Initial management:**
- ACLS/PALS as needed. Continuous cardiac monitoring, VS q2h.
- 2 large-bore IVs. Remove jewelry/tight clothing (anticipate swelling).
- Mark advancing edema edge with permanent marker + date/time. Repeat q30min initially, then q2h after CroFab started.
- Keep bitten extremity neutral initially; elevate after CroFab started.
- Monitor urine output and color. NPO until stabilized.
- IV NS bolus (rhabdomyolysis common). Pediatric: 20 mL/kg. Avoid large volumes in small infants.
- Pain: IV opioids initially ‚Üí PO as tolerated. **NO NSAIDs**.
- Update tetanus if needed.
- **No prophylactic antibiotics.**

**Labs:** CBC, PT/INR, PTT, fibrinogen, electrolytes, BUN/Cr, LFTs, CK, type & screen. EKG/CXR if cardiac history. Head CT if AMS/severe HA.

**CroFab dosing (give for any signs of envenomation):**
- **Dry bite:** Observe 8 hours; repeat labs before discharge. No CroFab.
- **Initial dose: 4‚Äì6 vials IV.** No pediatric dose adjustment.
- Reconstitute each vial with 18mL NS; rock gently 10 minutes; add all to 250mL NS bag.
- Infuse at 50 mL/hr for first 10 minutes (watch for allergic reaction) ‚Üí 250 mL/hr if tolerated.

**Assessing initial control (1 hour post-infusion):** Repeat labs. Initial control = swelling progressed <1 inch, pain decreasing, coags normalizing.
- **Not controlled:** Repeat 4‚Äì6 vials q2h until controlled.
- **Controlled:** Repeat labs q6h; reassess swelling/pain q2h. Contact toxicology/poison control (1-800-222-1222) for maintenance dosing discussion.

**Admit to MICU or PICU.** Observe ‚â•24 hours after initial control for delayed effects.

**Follow-up:** Repeat labs q3 days for 2 weeks. Avoid NSAIDs/aspirin and elective surgery/dental for 2 weeks. Non-weight bearing until painless.

**Complications:**
- Hemorrhage unresponsive to CroFab ‚Üí transfuse.
- Rhabdomyolysis ‚Üí aggressive IVF; dialysis if myoglobinuric renal failure.
- Compartment syndrome ‚Üí clinical diagnosis; compartment pressures recommended; consult Ortho or Plastics.

### üî¥ Emergency Release & Hemorrhage Protocol
**File:** `Kaiser Emergency Release & Hemorrhage Protocol - Urgent Need - 7-2013.doc`
**Date:** July 2013 (~13 years old) üî¥

**Emergency Release (uncrossmatched, urgent):**
- Blood Bank issues **up to 2 units RBC within 10 minutes** of phone call.
- FP and platelets only as requested.
- Blood Bank phone: **OMC ext. 42984 | FMC ext. 28080**

**Hemorrhage Protocol (massive transfusion):**
- Triggered by physician or charge nurse.
- Blood Bank issues **4 units RBC within 10 minutes.**
- Thaws 4 units FFP ‚Üí available within **45 minutes.**
- Issues 1 unit platelets pheresis (order STAT if unavailable).
- Blood Bank **keeps ahead 4 RBC + 4 plasma + 1 platelets** until protocol cancelled.
- The phone call to Blood Bank constitutes a physician order.

**Process:**
1. Call Blood Bank with: your name/extension, patient name/MRN, physician name, transfusion location, confirm 4 RBC + 4 FFP + 1 platelets.
2. Send Blood Bank specimen immediately (if not already in lab).
3. Complete Emergency Release/Hemorrhage Protocol Form (Attachment 1) ‚Äî bring to Blood Bank.
4. Physician signs Emergency Blood Release Waiver form when crisis subsides and returns to Blood Bank.
5. Place all orders in HealthConnect once stabilized.
6. Cancel protocol when hemorrhage subsides (charge nurse or communication person calls Blood Bank).
7. Conduct debriefing after crisis.

**Uncrossmatched blood types:** Group O (neg or pos depending on supply) OR ABO-compatible if current sample in Blood Bank with confirmed ABO/Rh.
