📊 HEART Score Interpretation

6-week MACE risk (death, MI, revascularization):

0–3 — Low Risk
MACE ~1.7%. Eligible for early discharge with outpatient follow-up if serial troponins negative. Shared decision-making discussion documented.


4–6 — Moderate Risk
MACE ~12–17%. Consider observation, stress testing, or cardiology consult. Not safe for routine discharge without further evaluation.


7–10 — High Risk
MACE ~50–65%. Early invasive strategy indicated. Cardiology consult, admission, likely catheterization.


⚠️ HEART score does not replace clinical judgment. Patients with STEMI, new LBBB, or hemodynamic instability require immediate intervention regardless of score.

📊 ABCD2 Interpretation

0–3 Low risk
Often outpatient pathway if evaluation is otherwise reassuring and follow-up is rapid.


4–5 Moderate risk
Consider admission vs expedited stroke/TIA clinic based on resources and exam/imaging.


6–7 High risk
High early stroke risk; admission/urgent stroke pathway strongly favored.

🧠 NIH Stroke Scale — 15 items, 0–42

Score each item by observed performance. Do not change scores after coaching the patient. UN (untestable) is allowed for motor items only and counts as 0.

—
Score all 15 items
NIHSS guides stroke severity, treatment eligibility, and outcomes.
📊 NIHSS Severity

0 — No stroke symptoms

1–4 — Minor stroke

5–15 — Moderate stroke

16–20 — Moderate–severe stroke

21–42 — Severe stroke


Use NIHSS in conjunction with last-known-well time, imaging, and contraindication review when assessing IV thrombolysis or thrombectomy eligibility. Document baseline and serial scores.

📊 Interpretation

Wells DVT Score interpretation:

≤1 — Low probability
DVT prevalence ~5%. D-dimer negative → DVT excluded. D-dimer positive → ultrasound.


2–6 — Moderate probability
DVT prevalence ~17%. Compression ultrasound. If negative + D-dimer negative → DVT excluded.


≥7 — High probability
DVT prevalence ~53%. Compression ultrasound regardless of D-dimer. If negative, repeat in 1 week or MRI.
